The Parenthesis Child

Published on 6th January 2025

We’ve lived in fragments for as long as I can remember.
Two worlds, two lives,
with me somewhere in the middle,
a bridge no one crosses.
Not together,
not apart,
just distant.

We never talk about the cracks.
Instead, we pretend the ground isn’t shifting beneath us.
We smile thinly,
say nothing,
and let the silence do the talking.

Affection is rationed,
a rare offering tossed like breadcrumbs.
A passing “love you” on a screen,
a solitary “x”
that feels more like punctuation than warmth.
Never a hug,
never an embrace to remind me
that I belong.

Most days, I feel like an afterthought.
Something you manage,
but never celebrate.
A ghost moving through your lives,
heard, maybe, but not really seen.
Am I the parenthesis in your story?
A reminder of something lost?
A disappointment you carry quietly?

I wonder what it would feel like
to be held without hesitation,
to hear my name spoken like a promise.
To feel that I am not just the remnants of something broken,
but someone you choose, again and again.

But here we are,
still locked in our patterns of distance,
bound by a silence
no one wants to break.

And I keep waiting,
invisible and unheard,
for something that never comes.

Jayden C
