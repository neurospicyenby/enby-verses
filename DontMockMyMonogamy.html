Don’t Mock My Monogamy

Published on 15th December 2024


Oh, you say monogamy’s a myth?
Like unicorns and affordable housing,
Like love without compromise,
Like queer folks without nuance.

Let me get this straight, or queerly bent:
Your polyamory stands tall
On the scaffolding of my monogamy’s “failure.”
I see you.
But don’t build your mansion
out of the rubble of my choices.

You laugh at the “normies,”
Call it heteronormative cosplay,
Like I’m clutching pearls and rosary beads,
When really, I’m clutching one hand.
One heart.
One love.
And yeah, that’s enough for me.

Your queerness doesn’t erase mine.
Your freedom doesn’t make me a cage.
We don’t need to splinter the rainbow
just because some of us found joy
in a single shade.

Oh, you’re evolved? Cool.
Your spreadsheets, your calendars,
Your Doodle polls to plan who gets Tuesday nights.
I’m glad that works.
But don’t turn my simplicity into your punchline.
Don’t paint my devotion
as a relic of oppression.

I see your ethics.
Radical, consensual,
But why does your liberation
sound like my limitation?
Why does my commitment feel
like a threat to your revolution?

I can love and still be queer.
I can choose one and still be here.
I am not vanilla;
I’m the whole bean…rich, layered, grounded.
And my love isn’t less
just because it fits in a smaller cup.

Your polyamory isn’t the problem.
My monogamy isn’t the solution.
We’re all just bodies and hearts
trying to find something that feels
Like home.

So don’t diminish me.
Don’t call me “brainwashed.”
Don’t say I’ve drunk the poison of conformity
because I’ve chosen a smaller table
to eat from,
but damn, the meal is good.

You do you.
I’ll do me.
And just maybe, we can stop
shouting over each other
long enough to see
That love doesn’t need
A gatekeeper or a glossary.

I’m monogamous.
I’m queer.
And babes, that’s not up for debate.

Jayden C
