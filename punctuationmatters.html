Punctuation Matters

Published on 15th October 2024


I stand here tonight,
Not to teach you rules, but to share a truth.
Punctuation matters.

The smallest marks, the tiniest strokes,
Can hold the weight of life,
Or the end of it.

Take the semicolon;
It lingers in the air between two thoughts,
Not quite a period, not quite a comma,
But a decision to continue.

It’s a pause.
A breath between sentences.
An unspoken agreement to go on,
Despite the urge to stop.

For some, that semicolon
Is not just grammar, but a lifeline.
A whispered symbol of survival,
An open wound, stitched just enough
To see another sentence form.

They choose the semicolon.
They choose to hold on,
In the space between despair and hope,
Between a full stop
And a second chance.

But the full stop,
The period,
Is a decision too.
It ends without hesitation,
It halts without remorse.

It’s definitive.
The end of the sentence,
The end of the story.
Nothing comes after.
And that’s the tragedy.
The period silences a voice
That still had more to say.
A sentence cut short,
A life unfinished.

But look,
In your hands,
In your words,
There is power.
Every pause, every punctuation,
Matters.

Let the semicolon remind you:
There is always more to write.
There is always more to say.
Life doesn’t have to be a single sentence.
You can pause, reflect, and still go on.

So choose your punctuation wisely.
Choose your life wisely.
In the face of full stops,
Choose the semicolon;
It’s not the end.
It’s just a pause.

And in that pause,
You just might find
A reason to keep writing.

Jayden C
