ADHD Paralysis

Published on 25th October 2024

I’m here.
But not here.
Body still, mind running—fast—faster—too fast.
Where do I…?
How do I…?
What was I supposed to…?
Wait.

It’s like—
There’s this fog,
Thick, dense, wrapped around my head.
I reach for a thought,
but it slips.
Another,
gone.
And the list?
Oh, the list is endless.

Start here.
No, there.
Wait, but what about…?
Doesn’t matter.
Can’t even tell which thread to pull,
Which domino to tap,
They all just sit there,
taunting.

Everything,
all at once.
Nothing,
at all.

And me,
in the middle,
Frozen.
Like the first step is always the hardest,
But also the second.
And the third.
And before I know it,
the whole day’s gone,
and I’ve been sitting here,
doing…
what?

The noise, it’s loud but… it’s silent.
I can’t explain.
Like static on a broken TV,
Buzzing, humming,
But no picture,
Just—white noise.

I could start.
I should start.
I should’ve started by now.
But—
Start what?
Where?
Pick a point, any point, but they all blur together.

Do this.
Or that.
But what about the thing from last week I didn’t finish?
It’s still sitting there,
Next to this new thing I haven’t touched.
How does anyone keep up?

I move to stand,
but my feet are glued,
To the floor, to the moment, to the weight of everything.
And my hands?
They twitch, wanting to grab something,
Anything,
But can’t find the thread.
Can’t find the start.
Where is the start?

And so, I sit,
Still,
In the chaos,
In the swirl of it all,
Waiting for a spark,
For something to click.
But it doesn’t.

It just spins,
and spins,
and I stay here,
staring at the nothing,
feeling everything.

Jayden C
