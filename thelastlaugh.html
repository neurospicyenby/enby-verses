The Last Laugh

Published on 22nd January 2025]

It comes not in the grey of sorrow,
but in the gold of the day well-spent.
The hum of glasses clinking,
laughter strung between us like garlands.
A night so full it feels endless,
but I know it ends.

They think the darkness comes
when the world caves in,
when tears swell and throats close.
But no
It can be in the bloom of happiness,
when my chest is a vessel brimming with light.

It whispers:
“This is it.
This is as good as it gets.”
And the thought slices through the warmth
like cold steel.
It’s not despair, but the fear of the turn,
the moment the applause dies.
The grin falters.
The emptiness unfurls again.
What if they see the other side,
the cracks I’ve painted over?

And in that fear, a fragile logic:
Go now, while they remember you
as bright and whole.
Go now, before they see
you are neither.

But somewhere in the din of that thought,
a thread of defiance flickers,
the voice that knows tomorrow brings
another laugh,
another sunrise.

Yet still, the pull is there,
not a scream,
but a quiet hum beneath the joy,
a reminder that even in the light,
shadows form.

Jayden C
