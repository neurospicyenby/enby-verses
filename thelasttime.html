The Last Time

Published on 1st November 2024

We never knew when it was the last time, did we?

The last time we hugged someone without thinking.
The last time we stayed up late, talking till dawn, laughing until our sides ached.
The last time we said, “See you later” and truly believed it.
We didn’t realise that was the final goodbye, did we?
Not then. Not in that moment.

The last time we wore our favourite jumper, before it unravelled.
The last time we ate at that café, ordered the usual, before it closed for good.
The last time we heard a song and danced like no one was watching,
because the truth is, no one was, except our younger selves.
They were watching, hoping we’d remember.

We didn’t realise when we were living those last times.
That last family dinner where every seat was filled.
The last time we saw a friend’s name pop up on our screen,
and answered without hesitation.
Or the last time we played that game we thought we’d always play.

The last time we held someone’s hand,
or felt the warmth of their laugh spill over us,
never knowing it would be the last time.

Funny how life does that, isn’t it?
Wraps moments in ordinary until they quietly slip away,
until they’re just memories,
tucked into corners of our minds we rarely visit.

We chase the firsts,
First love, first job, first adventure
But it’s the last times that catch us off guard.
The final heartbeat of a moment,
gone before we’ve even heard its echo.
Maybe that’s what makes them precious.
The fact that we didn’t know.
We couldn’t know.
And maybe, in that not knowing,
we gave them the space to be perfect.

So here’s to the last times,
the ones we didn’t recognise.
For living so quietly,
for leaving so softly.
And for reminding us,
that every moment deserves to be cherished.
Because we’ll never know
when it’s the last time.
Jayden C
