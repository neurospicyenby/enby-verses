They're Winning

Published on 10th December 2024



They say,
“Don’t let them win.”
But he already did.
In that brief moment,
a shove that didn’t quite land,
a trip that almost did,
and then the word:
“Fag.”

I didn’t freeze for long.
Just a second.
Long enough to feel it hit,
like a punch I didn’t see coming.
Then I hurried away,
head down,
heart racing,
pride shattered,
their laughter ripping through the air
like glass.

It bounced off the walls,
off my back,
off my years of trying
to stand tall.
And in that moment,
it didn’t matter how far I’ve come.
I wasn’t a person.
I was a target.
A punchline.
A reminder that, to them,
I’m still less than human.

They say,
“Don’t let them win.”
But how?
How do you not let them win
when the fight never fucking ends?
When every step forward
feels like another battle waiting,
like the world is rigged
to shove us down again?

I’m so tired.
Tired of dodging their words,
their hands,
their sneers.
Tired of pretending I’m not angry.
Tired of having to explain,
again and again,
that this shit still happens.
That this world isn’t better yet.

This isn’t just about me.
It’s about all of us,
the queer kid terrified to speak up,
the trans woman pretending not to hear,
the ones who couldn’t hold on anymore
because the weight of it all
was too much.

They say,
“The fight is over.”
How dare they?
How dare they tell me it’s over
when the word still cuts like a knife,
when their laughter still burns,
when I still carry this exhaustion,
this rage,
this fucking truth
that no one wants to see?

I’m not here to ask for pity.
I’m not here to ask for permission
to be angry.
I’m here to say
I’m done.
Done running.
Done carrying this alone.
Done pretending
that I’m not fucking tired.

Because as long as we’re still running,
as long as we’re still begging
to just exist in peace,
they’re winning.
And I don’t know how long
I can keep this fight going.

But for now, I will.
Because if I don’t,
who will?

Jayden C
