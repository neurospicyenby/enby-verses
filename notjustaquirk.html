Not Just A Quirk

Published on 14th October 2024


You say,
Everyone’s a little bit ADHD,
Like I’m making excuses, like it’s a trendy thing to be.
Like it’s just a scattered mind,
Like we’re all the same kind.

Nah, I’m not buying your cheap diagnosis.
You see, I live this.
I fight the fog of thoughts that whirl in constant motion,
Drowning in the sea of half-started ideas,
Chasing time like it’s a thing that will stand still for me.

This isn’t just a “focus issue.”
This isn’t just “get organised.”
You think if I could just write a to-do list,
Suddenly everything would fall into line?
That if I just tried harder,
Maybe I’d stop losing track of time,
Stop the day from slipping away in a million unfinished things?

Tell me,
Do you feel your brain trip over itself at every turn,
Your mind like a web of static,
Fighting just to hold one thought for more than a fleeting second?
Does your focus slip through your fingers
Like trying to catch smoke with bare hands?

Don’t tell me it’s a superpower.
Don’t slap a cape on this chaos and call it a gift.
I don’t need to be your superhero,
I don’t need to turn this mess into magic for your convenience.

Yeah, I get it.
We’ve got moments; sparks of brilliance,
But the light flickers out just as fast,
Leaving us with unfinished sentences,
Half-lived lives,
Wishing the good parts lasted longer.

No, this isn’t a gift.
It’s a disability.
You think that’s too harsh?
I live with the truth.
I’ve seen potential leak out the cracks,
Watched it slip away because I can’t keep up.
And I’m tired of people dressing it up,
Tired of pretending it’s a secret strength,
When it feels more like running on legs that just won’t stop shaking.

So don’t tell me everyone’s a little bit ADHD.
Don’t make it cute, or quirky, or fun.
Because this is hard,
And not everyone’s carrying this weight.

So let me say it clearly.
This isn’t your superpower.
This is my struggle, my fight, my life.

Jayden C
