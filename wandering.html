Wandering

Published on 30th October 2024

I walk these streets like I’ve walked them a thousand times before.
Same paths, same corners, same faces I don’t know.
The city hums around me,
but I’m drifting, floating, untethered.
Like a ghost that no one sees.

There’s a weight in my chest that no one can carry.
Not here. Not tonight.
It’s just me and these endless roads,
paved with stories that aren’t mine.
Stories I’ll never know,
and I wonder,
where do I fit in this?

I see them, though.
The couples.
Laughing, holding hands, their smiles like secrets.
It’s the little things,
brushing fingers, stolen glances.
Things I used to want,
but now I don’t know if they were ever real for me.
Or just something I saw in films,
something other people get to have.
I catch reflections in shop windows.
Strangers with perfect hair, perfect clothes,
like they stepped out of another world.
A world I keep knocking on the door of
but no one ever answers.

And God, the longing.
It sneaks up on you, doesn’t it?
One minute you’re just walking,
the next you’re drowning in it.
It’s like a storm that swirls inside,
a longing for someone to notice,
to really see you,
not just pass you by.
I’m tired of being single,
of the echo that comes with it.
No good morning texts.
No arms around me when the world gets heavy.
Just me, carrying it all,
alone.
Alone in a city full of people.
Beautiful people.

I wonder if they feel it too.
This ache.
If they see the loneliness in my eyes
like I see it in theirs.
Or are they too busy, too happy,
too wrapped up in their own love stories to notice?

I keep walking,
because what else can I do?
Step after step, through streets that know my name
but offer me nothing.
Just another night, just another city,
just another reminder that something is missing.
Maybe one day, I’ll walk these same streets and not feel this way. Maybe one day, I’ll be the one with a hand to hold, laughing at nothing, smiling at everything. But for now, I’m just here, wandering, wondering, waiting.
Jayden C
