Silent Blue Ticks

Published: October 24th 2024

I’m the ghost in your phone,
Not the spooky kind,
More like the “I meant to text back, I swear” kind.
I see the message, think of the perfect reply,
But then… it fades,
Like fog in the morning.

It’s not you. It’s me.
And my ADHD brain,
Running a marathon through a maze,
Each thought tripping over the next.
I want to say something, really,
But then…
Oh, look, something shiny.

Days turn into weeks,
Conversations fade into “sorry, I forgot”
And I don’t even know how to pick them back up.
I’m not avoiding you,
I’m just tangled in my own head,
Like headphones in a pocket.

You call it ghosting,
But I call it accidental stealth mode,
Disappearing without meaning to,
Leaving a trail of half-finished thoughts,
Promising myself “I’ll respond later.”
Later never comes.

But it’s funny, right?
How I can vanish so easily
And still think I’m in the conversation,
Like I’m texting back in some parallel universe.
Sorry about that.
Maybe I’ll respond tomorrow.
Or maybe not.

Jayden C
